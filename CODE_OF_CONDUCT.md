# Code of Conduct

EazePay engineering exists to move money safely on behalf of consumers
who trusted us with their financial lives. That obligation shapes how
we work with each other.

## Our standards

Behavior we expect:

- **Substance over politics.** Disagree with the work, not the person.
  When you disagree, propose a better answer.
- **Privacy first.** Never share customer NPI in a chat, a screenshot,
  a ticket, or a slide. Never. There is no exception for "just for
  debugging".
- **Document the trade-off.** If you made a choice that surprised
  someone, write the ADR or the comment that explains why.
- **Take the call.** On-call is a shared responsibility. If you're
  paged, you respond. If you can't, you find cover.
- **Mentor down, push up.** Senior engineers explain. Juniors ask.
  Anyone, regardless of title, can flag a risk and expect to be heard.
- **Bring solutions, not just problems.** "X is broken" is a start;
  "X is broken and here are two options" is finished.

Behavior we will not tolerate:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind.
- Sharing customer data outside its authorized use.
- Reaching around our access controls or audit trail "to move faster".
- Falsifying or omitting evidence in a postmortem, a SOC 2 control, or
  a regulator response.
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith.

## Reporting

If you see something, say something. In order of preference:

1. **Speak to the people involved.** Most issues resolve at that layer.
2. **Speak to your manager** or the affected team's manager.
3. **`conduct@eazepay.com`** — read by the Head of People and the
   General Counsel. Confidential.
4. **Anonymous tip line** — link maintained in the People wiki. Use
   this when the people above are the problem.

All reports are taken seriously and investigated. Retaliation is itself
a violation and is grounds for termination.

## Enforcement

The Head of People and the General Counsel jointly decide outcomes.
Consequences range from a private conversation, to a written warning,
to suspension, to termination — proportionate to the conduct and the
record. Regulated-services roles have additional regulatory
notification obligations that may apply.

## Scope

This Code applies in the workplace, in any EazePay-affiliated venue
(off-sites, conferences, customer meetings), and in public channels
where you represent EazePay. It applies to employees, contractors,
interns, and anyone with commit access to this repository.

---

This Code is the floor, not the ceiling. The character of this team
is built by what we do every day.
